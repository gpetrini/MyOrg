** Background and motivation

- This is a project written by Luna and Canul-Reich and submited to Faepex (More Women in Research).
- Increase in computational power enlarges the uses of ML methods
- Without proper training and interpretation, GenAI might lead to miss-applications of AI tools
- AI and ML have a high performance, but faces a low level of interpretability
  - The explainability of those tools might be more relevant than results per se in the public debate
  - An parameter in a complex AI model has no interpretability (at odds with econometrics)

** Supporting Ideas and hypothesis

- Hunger and food insecurity are persistent problems in Latin America
  - There is a recent fluke in the increase of hunger
  - This intesifies (and is intesified) by the strong inequality
- Brazil and Mexico share similarity in terms of the trajectory of nutritional and food insecurity

** Purpose, Relevance, and Contribution

- Food and nutricional insecurity
- Use interpretable ML and explainable AI to generate *evidence*
- Scope: Bolsa Família as a case study
- Targets generating evidences assisting public policies
- Originality: Analysis of income transfer program and IAN via ML
- Objectives: Evaluate the potential benefits of AI in the production of interpretable empirical evidence
  - Mapping the AIs being used for public-policies guidance decisions
  - Classify the proposed methods among the existing alternatives in the literature
  - How changes in the investments in the PBF are associated with IAN in Brazilian municipalities? (PNADc)

  - Explainable models needs more structure to be interpretable
    - LIME allows the interpretation of explainable models
    - It only generates approximations about the model predictions
    - A kriging meta-model is an explainable model by means of surface responses
